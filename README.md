# 🗄️ SQL Business Insights Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project is an **Advanced SQL Data Analytics Project** focused on extracting meaningful business insights from a relational database using SQL.

The project demonstrates how SQL can be used for data exploration, business reporting, performance analysis, customer segmentation, product analysis, and sales trend analysis.

The main goal of this project is to analyze business data and generate insights that can support data-driven decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Objective

The objective of this project is to answer key business questions using SQL, such as:

* What are the overall sales and revenue trends?
* Which products and categories perform the best?
* Who are the most valuable customers?
* How does business performance change over time?
* Which customer segments contribute the most to revenue?
* What are the month-over-month and year-over-year trends?
* How can SQL be used to generate business-ready insights?

## 📊 Analysis Performed

### 1. Database Exploration

Initial exploration was performed to understand the database structure, tables, columns, and available data.

This includes:

* Checking available tables
* Understanding column names and data types
* Exploring relationships between tables
* Reviewing sample records

---

### 2. Measures and Metrics Analysis

Important business KPIs were calculated using SQL.

Key metrics include:

* Total sales
* Total revenue
* Total customers
* Total products
* Average order value
* Total quantity sold

---

### 3. Time-Based Trend Analysis

Sales and revenue trends were analyzed over time to understand business growth and seasonality.

This includes:

* Monthly sales trends
* Yearly revenue trends
* Revenue changes over time
* Sales performance by date

---

### 4. Cumulative Analysis

Cumulative calculations were performed to understand long-term business performance.

This includes:

* Running total revenue
* Running total sales
* Moving average analysis
* Cumulative business growth

---

### 5. Performance Analysis

Performance analysis was carried out to compare current values with previous periods.

This includes:

* Month-over-month sales comparison
* Year-over-year revenue comparison
* Product performance comparison
* Customer purchase behavior comparison

---

### 6. Part-to-Whole Analysis

Part-to-whole analysis was used to understand the contribution of each category, product, or customer segment to overall business performance.

This includes:

* Category contribution to total revenue
* Product contribution percentage
* Customer segment contribution
* Revenue share analysis

---

### 7. Customer Segmentation

Customers were segmented based on their purchase behavior and revenue contribution.

This helps identify:

* High-value customers
* Regular customers
* Low-value customers
* Customer groups based on spending patterns

---

### 8. Product Analysis

Product-level analysis was performed to identify the best-performing and low-performing products.

This includes:

* Top products by revenue
* Top products by quantity sold
* Product ranking
* Product category performance
